The Colosseum

Explore the history of Rome's ancient amphitheater

Overview

The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheater, is one of the most famous landmarks in Rome and a symbol of ancient Roman engineering and architecture. Located in the heart of Rome, Italy, the Colosseum was built between 70 and 80 AD under the emperors Vespasian and Titus. It was originally used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, animal hunts, and mock naval battles. With a seating capacity of around 50,000 spectators, it was the largest amphitheater in the Roman Empire and remains the largest amphitheater in the world today.

The Colosseum is an architectural marvel, built from concrete and stone, and it features a series of arches and columns that showcase the engineering prowess of ancient Romans. Over the centuries, the Colosseum has suffered from earthquakes, fires, and stone robbers, but it still stands as a testament to the grandeur of Roman culture.

Top Things to See and Do at the Colosseum

The Colosseum is one of the most visited monuments in the world, attracting millions of tourists each year. Here are some of the top things to see and do when visiting the Colosseum:

  • Explore the Interior: Walk through the ancient corridors and passageways that once hosted gladiators and wild animals. Visit the underground chambers where gladiators prepared for battle and animals were kept before their appearance in the arena.
  • Visit the Arena Floor: While the original wooden floor has long been gone, the stone remains allow you to imagine the grandeur of the gladiatorial contests that took place here. Standing on the arena floor gives you a sense of the scale and history of the Colosseum.
  • The Upper Levels: Climb to the upper levels of the Colosseum for breathtaking views of the Roman Forum and the city of Rome. The top levels offer a panoramic view of the Colosseum and its surroundings, making it a great spot for photography.
  • Colosseum Audio Guides and Tours: To gain a deeper understanding of the history of the Colosseum, consider joining a guided tour or using an audio guide. These tours provide insights into the building's architecture, history, and the events that took place there.
  • The Colosseum at Night: The Colosseum is often illuminated at night, offering a magical atmosphere as it lights up against the Roman skyline. The evening tours are less crowded and offer a more intimate experience of this ancient wonder.

Best Time to Visit the Colosseum

The Colosseum is a popular tourist attraction, and as such, it can get quite crowded, especially during peak seasons. To make the most of your visit, consider the following:

  • Best Time of Year: The best time to visit the Colosseum is during the spring (April to June) and fall (September to November). The weather is pleasant, and there are fewer tourists compared to the busy summer months.
  • Best Time of Day: Early mornings and late afternoons are ideal times to visit, as the Colosseum tends to be less crowded and the light is perfect for photography. If you visit during peak hours, you may have to deal with long lines.
  • Skip the Line: To avoid long waits, it’s a good idea to book tickets online in advance or opt for a skip-the-line guided tour.

How to Get to the Colosseum

The Colosseum is located in the city center of Rome, easily accessible by public transport and on foot. Here are some ways to get there:

  • By Metro: The Colosseum is located just a short walk from the Colosseo metro station (Line B), which makes it very easy to reach from other parts of the city.
  • By Bus: Several buses stop near the Colosseum, including routes 75, 87, and 117. Check local schedules for the best bus routes from your location.
  • By Foot: If you are staying in the city center, the Colosseum is within walking distance from other major landmarks like the Roman Forum, Palatine Hill, and the Piazza Venezia.

Conclusion

Whether you're fascinated by its history, architecture, or just want to experience one of the world’s most iconic landmarks, the Colosseum offers something for everyone.
