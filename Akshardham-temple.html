Akshardham Temple

A Glimpse of India's Rich Culture and Spirituality

About Akshardham Temple

Akshardham Temple, also known as the Swaminarayan Akshardham Temple, is a magnificent Hindu temple located in Delhi, India. It was inaugurated in 2005 and is dedicated to Bhagwan Swaminarayan, a spiritual leader and founder of the Swaminarayan tradition.

Architectural Splendor

The temple's central monument is made of pink sandstone and white marble, showcasing intricate carvings of deities, animals, and scenes from Hindu mythology. The entire structure is an example of Vastu Shastra (traditional Indian architecture).

Attractions

  • Bhoomi Mandap: The temple's entrance hall with stunning exhibits on Indian history and spirituality.
  • Yagnapurush Kund: The largest stepwell in the world, featuring a sacred water tank.
  • Swaramandir: The main temple housing the deity Swaminarayan in its sanctum.
  • Boat Ride: A mesmerizing boat ride that takes visitors through 10,000 years of India's history and culture.

Visiting Information

The Akshardham Temple is open every day except Monday. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful and serene experience, soaking in the spirituality and grandeur of the temple.

Timings: 9:30 AM to 6:30 PM
