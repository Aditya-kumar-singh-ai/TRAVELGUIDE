Leaning Tower of Pisa

Overview

The Leaning Tower of Pisa (Torre Pendente di Pisa) is a freestanding bell tower of the cathedral in Pisa, Italy. It is renowned worldwide for its unintended tilt, which was caused by unstable foundation soil. Standing at approximately 57 meters (186 feet) tall, the tower is one of the most recognized and iconic landmarks in the world.

Located in the Piazza dei Miracoli, the Leaning Tower of Pisa is part of the Cathedral of Pisa, the Baptistery, and the Camposanto Monumentale, all of which are UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

History

The construction of the Leaning Tower began in 1173 and took nearly 200 years to complete due to various interruptions, including wars and financial difficulties. The tilt started during construction when the foundation, built on soft, marshy ground, was unable to support the weight of the tower.

Despite the tilt, work continued through various stages, with multiple attempts to correct the lean. The tower was completed in the 14th century, but its tilt remained a prominent feature. Over the centuries, engineers have worked to stabilize the tower, and in 2001, the tilt was reduced to a safe level through extensive restoration work.

Architecture

The Leaning Tower of Pisa is built in the Romanesque style, using white and grey marble. It has eight stories, including the chamber for the bells. The tower is composed of a circular stone structure, with a series of open arches on each floor that allow natural light to enter. The tower has a distinct architectural elegance, despite its infamous tilt.

Visitor Information

The Leaning Tower of Pisa is open to visitors year-round, but it's recommended to check for specific visiting hours as they may change due to weather conditions or maintenance work. Visitors can climb to the top of the tower for a spectacular view of Pisa and the surrounding area.

To climb the tower, visitors must purchase tickets, and due to the narrow staircase, access is limited. It's recommended to book tickets in advance, especially during peak tourist seasons. Visitors can also explore the surrounding Piazza dei Miracoli, which includes the cathedral, the Baptistery, and the Camposanto Monumentale, all of which are historically significant and architecturally stunning.
